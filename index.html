About

Dr. Jagannath Lenka is a distinguished Professor of Economics at North Orissa University, Baripada, Odisha. With decades of academic excellence, he has dedicated his career to advancing economic research and education in India.

His research spans critical areas including regional economics, agricultural development, and human development studies. Dr. Lenka's work on rural farmer indebtedness and crop diversification has contributed significantly to understanding agrarian challenges in India.

As a passionate educator, Dr. Lenka has mentored numerous students and served as Head of Department, shaping the next generation of economists and researchers.

Research Focus

Agricultural & Rural Economics

Dr. Lenka's pioneering research on rural farmer household indebtedness across major Indian states has been widely cited and has informed policy discussions on agricultural finance and farmer welfare.

His work on crop diversification in Orissa employs advanced econometric techniques to analyze agricultural transformation and its implications for food security and rural livelihoods.

Regional Development

Through extensive analysis of per capita income convergence across Indian states, Dr. Lenka has contributed to understanding regional inequality dynamics in the post-reform era.

Social Development

His collaborative work on gender perspectives in disaster management highlights the intersection of economics with social vulnerability and resilience.
